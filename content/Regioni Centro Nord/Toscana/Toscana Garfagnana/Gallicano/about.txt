<p>Gallicano, a lively village in Tuscany’s Garfagnana, nestles in the Serchio Valley, known for its community spirit and Apennine backdrop. Its Chiesa di San Jacopo, with frescoes from the 14th century, and annual Palio dei Carretti celebrate local traditions. Surrounded by chestnut groves, Gallicano is a hub for foraging and outdoor pursuits like hiking to Monte Rovaio. Its cuisine, featuring spelt soups and grilled meats, embodies Garfagnana’s rustic flavors, making it a warm, authentic Tuscan stop.</p>
