<p>Montefegatesi, a remote hamlet in Tuscany’s Garfagnana, clings to the Apennines at 842 meters, offering solitude and stunning views. Its stone houses and tiny Chiesa di San Frediano reflect its medieval origins, while chestnut forests and hiking trails surround the village. Known for its chestnut festival and homemade farricelli pasta, Montefegatesi embodies rustic simplicity. A haven for those seeking unspoiled nature and authentic Tuscan life, it’s a perfect escape from the region’s busier tourist hubs.</p>
