<p>San Pellegrino in Alpe, a high-altitude village straddling Tuscany and Emilia-Romagna’s Apennines, is a spiritual and scenic gem at 1,525 meters. Its sanctuary, dedicated to saints Pellegrino and Bianco, draws pilgrims, while the Ethnographic Museum showcases mountain life. Offering panoramic views of the Apuan Alps, it’s a haven for hikers tackling the Via Matildica. Known for its crisp air and hearty dishes like polenta with mushrooms, San Pellegrino’s remote beauty and sacred aura make it a unique alpine retreat.</p>
