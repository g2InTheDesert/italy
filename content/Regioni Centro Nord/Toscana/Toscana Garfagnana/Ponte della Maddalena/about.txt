<p>Ponte della Maddalena, near Bagni di Lucca in Tuscany’s Garfagnana, is a medieval marvel known as the “Devil’s Bridge” for its dramatic arches spanning the Serchio River. Built in the 11th century, its steep humpbacked design is an engineering feat, steeped in legend. Surrounded by lush hills, it’s a photogenic landmark on the Via Francigena, drawing photographers and historians. The bridge’s serene setting and nearby village life offer a glimpse into Tuscany’s medieval soul and timeless beauty.</p>
