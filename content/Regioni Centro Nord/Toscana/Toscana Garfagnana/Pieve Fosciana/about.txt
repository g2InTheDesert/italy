<p>Pieve Fosciana, a serene village in Tuscany’s Garfagnana, is named for its ancient parish church, the Pieve di San Giovanni Battista, a Romanesque treasure from the 11th century. Nestled in the Serchio Valley, it’s surrounded by vineyards and olive groves, ideal for cycling and agriturismo stays. Local festivals celebrate farro and wine, reflecting the region’s agricultural roots. Pieve Fosciana’s quiet charm, historic architecture, and proximity to the Apennines make it a delightful stop for travelers seeking rural Tuscany.</p>
