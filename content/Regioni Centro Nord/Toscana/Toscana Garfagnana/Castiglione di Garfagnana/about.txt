<p>Castiglione di Garfagnana, a fortified hilltop village in Tuscany’s Garfagnana, boasts medieval walls and Apennine views. Its 13th-century walls and Rocca castle reflect its defensive past, while the Romanesque Chiesa di San Michele anchors the historic center. Surrounded by chestnut forests, it’s a hub for hiking and foraging, with local specialties like farro soup. Annual events like the medieval festival bring history alive. Castiglione’s rugged beauty and authentic cuisine make it a charming base for exploring Tuscany’s wild north.</p>
