<p>Pieve Santo Stefano, a tranquil town in Tuscany’s Tiber Valley, is known as the “City of Diaries” for its National Diary Archive, preserving Italians’ personal stories. Its historic center, rebuilt after WWII, features the Romanesque Collegiata di Santo Stefano and quaint piazzas. Surrounded by the Apennines, it’s a gateway to hiking trails and spiritual sites like La Verna. Local cuisine, like crostini di fegato, reflects Tuscan simplicity. Pieve Santo Stefano’s unique cultural mission and serene setting make it a hidden gem for introspective travelers.</p>
