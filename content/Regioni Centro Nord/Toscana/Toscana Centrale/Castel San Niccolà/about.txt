<p>Castel San Niccolò, a medieval stronghold in Tuscany’s Casentino Valley, perches above the Arno River, offering sweeping views and historic charm. Its 13th-century castle ruins and quaint stone village evoke the region’s feudal past, while the surrounding forests invite quiet hikes. The annual Sagra del Fungo celebrates local porcini, paired with polenta and Chianti. Close to spiritual sites like La Verna, Castel San Niccolò is a serene escape for those seeking Tuscany’s unspoiled landscapes and authentic traditions.</p>
