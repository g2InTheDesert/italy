<p>San Gimignano, Tuscany’s “Medieval Manhattan,” is famed for its 14 surviving towers, relics of its 13th-century prosperity. A UNESCO site, its Piazza della Cisterna and Collegiata, with vivid frescoes, draw visitors, while Vernaccia di San Gimignano wine pairs with saffron-infused dishes. The town’s skyline, rising from rolling vineyards, is iconic, and its artisan shops sell ceramics and leather. San Gimignano’s frozen-in-time ambiance and panoramic views make it a quintessential stop for those exploring Tuscany’s hill towns.</p>
