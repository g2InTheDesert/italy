<p>Castiglione d’Orcia, a hilltop village in Tuscany’s UNESCO-listed Val d’Orcia, captivates with its medieval fortress and sweeping views. The Rocca di Tentennano, a 13th-century castle, dominates the skyline, offering panoramas of cypress-lined hills. The village’s stone streets lead to the Romanesque Chiesa di Santa Maria Maddalena, while nearby Bagni San Filippo boasts thermal springs. Known for its olive oil and proximity to Montalcino’s vineyards, Castiglione d’Orcia is a serene retreat for lovers of Tuscan landscapes and history.</p>
