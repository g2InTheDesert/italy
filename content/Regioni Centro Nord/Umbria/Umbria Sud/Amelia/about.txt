<p>Amelia, one of Umbria’s oldest towns, boasts 3,000-year-old Cyclopean walls from its Umbrian origins. Perched above the Tiber Valley, its Roman gate, Porta Romana, and 11th-century Duomo dominate the skyline. The Palazzo Farrattini and archaeological museum showcase its layered past. Amelia’s olive oil, among Italy’s finest, and dishes like ciriole alla ternana shine. A quiet gem with Etruscan, Roman, and medieval roots, Amelia offers history and authenticity for travelers seeking Umbria’s soul.</p>
