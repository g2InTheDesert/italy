<p>Todi, a medieval Umbrian hilltop town, is a masterpiece of balance, with its Piazza del Popolo among Italy’s most photogenic squares. The Gothic Palazzo del Popolo and Santa Maria della Consolazione, a Renaissance church, are architectural gems. Known as the “world’s most livable city,” Todi’s winding streets offer artisan shops and views of the Tiber Valley. Its cuisine, featuring truffles and wild boar, delights, while festivals add vibrancy. Todi’s timeless elegance makes it a romantic and cultural gem.</p>
