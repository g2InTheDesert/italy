<p>Bastardo, a small village in Umbria’s Giano dell’Umbria, is a quiet agricultural hub surrounded by olive groves and vineyards. Its name, derived from a medieval inn, belies its peaceful charm, with stone houses and the simple Chiesa di San Michele. Known for its high-quality olive oil, Bastardo hosts festivals celebrating Umbrian harvests. An agriturismo haven, it offers farm stays with homemade strangozzi and Montefalco wines, ideal for travelers seeking rural tranquility near Assisi and Spoleto.</p>
