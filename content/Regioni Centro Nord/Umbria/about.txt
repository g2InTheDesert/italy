<p>Umbria, known as Italy’s “green heart,” is a land of rolling hills, medieval towns, and spiritual significance. Assisi, the birthplace of St. Francis, draws pilgrims to its stunning basilica, while Perugia, the capital, blends Etruscan roots with a vibrant university scene. Towns like Orvieto, with its majestic cathedral, and Gubbio, steeped in medieval mystique, add to Umbria’s allure. The region’s truffles, olive oils, and Sagrantino wines highlight its culinary prowess, making Umbria a serene haven for slow travel and introspection.</p>
