<p>Enna, Sicily’s “navel,” sits at 900 meters, offering sweeping views across the island’s heartland. Known as the “Belvedere of Sicily,” its Castello di Lombardia, one of Italy’s largest medieval fortresses, and the Duomo, with its baroque facade, highlight its storied past. Enna’s Holy Week processions are among Italy’s most evocative, blending faith and tradition. Surrounded by wheat fields and almond groves, Enna’s rustic cuisine and elevated serenity make it a captivating stop for those exploring Sicily’s interior.</p>
