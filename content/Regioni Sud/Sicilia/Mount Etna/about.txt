<p>Mount Etna, Europe’s tallest active volcano, dominates Sicily’s eastern skyline, a UNESCO World Heritage site revered for its geological drama and fertile slopes. Its lunar-like craters and lava fields draw adventurers for guided treks, while vineyards at its base produce acclaimed Etna Rosso wines. Towns like Zafferana Etnea offer honey and pistachio specialties, and cable cars provide access to higher altitudes. Etna’s blend of raw natural power, scenic beauty, and culinary riches makes it a magnet for nature lovers and epicureans.</p>
